Postdoctoral Fellow, University of Alaska Fairbanks

I'm a planetary scientist interested in quantitative studies of volcanic (and sometimes other geologic) processes on Venus, Mars, Earth, and the Moon using radar remote sensing, theoretical modeling techniques, and sometimes field investigations.

I'm primarily interested in understanding how the inherent differences between the rocky planets and moons (in planetary radii, geodynamic evolution, atmospheric conditions, etc.) impact physical processes related to volcanism. I also dabble a little bit in studies of mass transport processes under ambient conditions that can occur on planets like Mars and Venus. My current research predominantly involves studies of Venusian geology and geophysics.

I recently finished my PhD at the University of Arizona's Lunar and Planetary Lab and started a postdoctoral position at the University of Alaska Fairbanks Geophysical Institute .
